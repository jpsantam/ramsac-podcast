BEAT 30:25–2:15The conversation

LouLet's deal with the objection: you sell this. "You need testing, and conveniently, we sell testing."
RobFair — same energy as the fire brigade telling you to check your smoke alarm. Annoying that they'd benefit, doesn't make them wrong. If we find nothing, you've paid for confidence instead of fear. If we do, it's on your terms, not a criminal's — a ranked list, not a panic: what's wrong, what it costs, what to fix first with the budget you've got.
LouNo pitch, just the pattern.
RobExactly why it's written that way.
LouAnd "too small to be a target" — does that hold?
RobBackwards, if anything — it's about whether you run digital systems and hold data, not whether you're interesting. And it's not just criminals asking anymore: insurers want evidence of testing at renewal, and plenty of bigger clients ask before they'll even sign a contract.
LouSo it's stopped being optional.
BEAT 42:15–2:35What ramsac sees
LouNo pitch, just the pattern — what are you actually seeing out there?
