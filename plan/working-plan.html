01 The brief, in one page

Pulled from the Granola notes on the Ramsac Podcast meeting, so the rest of this plan can be checked against it.

Goal — an AI-produced podcast that sounds like a natural two-person conversation: educational, non-technical, business-case led. Ramsac branding stays subtle, with a soft call-to-action only at the end of each episode.

Audience — SMBs (50–500 users) and C-suite readers, weighted toward two buckets: charities/not-for-profit, and professional services.

Verticals — Cybersecurity (topical, already the strongest performer on the blog), AI (an evolving, fast-growing audience), Microsoft 365 (an "underused features" angle — value people are already paying for and not using).

Format — miniseries of ~5 bite-sized episodes per theme, each vertical building as one coherent block rather than standalone episodes.

Constraint — Ramsac's site migrates in-house on 13 July; new written content is on hold until then. This plan is unaffected — podcast scripting and audio production don't depend on the CMS — but publishing/embedding on ramsac.com should be sequenced after the migration.
Content policy — no polarizing subject matter: no politics, geopolitics, or religion, even where a source blog post touches on it. Where a blog post's hook is geopolitical or otherwise charged (e.g. the original Middle East cyber-risk article), reframe the episode around the underlying, apolitical business or technical mechanism rather than the current-events angle — the stakes still land without the flashpoint. Applied retroactively to Cybersecurity EP01 below; worth a final pass over the rest of the season map before scripting, since a source article's framing can shift after this plan was first drafted.

02 Production pipeline & tools

The Granola action item was: pull the blog, find the gaps, draft scripts. This is the pipeline that turns a blog post into a published, on-brand episode.

01 · Source

Mine existing blog posts per vertical

Each episode maps to one or two existing articles (see miniseries tables below) rather than starting from a blank page — reuses content Ramsac already knows performs, and keeps the podcast consistent with what's published on the site.

ramsac.com/blogCategory pages by vertical
02 · Script

Convert the article into a two-host conversation

Not a read-aloud of the blog. One host plays the informed generalist asking the questions a business owner would actually ask; the other plays the specialist who answers in plain language, with one concrete example per episode. Claude drafts from the source article + the episode brief below; a human pass checks accuracy, tone, that no client-identifying detail leaks from case-study material, and that nothing in the content-policy callout above has slipped back in.

Claude (script draft)Human review — Louise
03 · Voice

Generate two consistent host voices

Two options, different tradeoffs: NotebookLM's Audio Overview generates a two-host conversation directly from source docs fastest, but voice/pacing isn't fully controllable episode-to-episode, which matters for a recognizable, returning show. ElevenLabs (or Play.ht) with two cloned/designed voices costs more setup but keeps the same two "hosts" recognizable across all 15 episodes — recommended, since recurring hosts are part of what makes a miniseries feel like a series.

ElevenLabs Studio (recommended)NotebookLM Audio Overview (fast alternative)
04 · Assemble

Add intro, outro, and the soft CTA

A fixed, short intro/outro bed (same music, same 8–10 second cold open) is what makes fifteen AI-generated episodes read as one show rather than fifteen one-offs. The CTA line sits after the recap, before the outro music, and is voiced by the host — not a separate ad-style insert.

Descript / AudacityRoyalty-free intro bed
05 · QA

Full listen-through before release

AI voice + AI script both need a human check for factual accuracy (dates, product names, figures), mispronunciations, and brand tone — this is the step most likely to get skipped under time pressure and shouldn't be.

Louise sign-off
06 · Publish

Distribute and embed

Host on Spotify for Podcasters or Buzzsprout for the RSS feed (Apple/Spotify/YouTube Music in one submission); embed the player on the relevant ramsac.com blog post once the site migration lands. Cover art built once per miniseries, not once per episode, in Canva.

07 · Repurpose

Cut the transcript back into marketing assets

The generated transcript becomes a short LinkedIn post per episode and a "listen to this episode" callout on the source blog post — closing the loop back to the article it came from.

03 Episode format & template

Same skeleton every episode (8–12 minutes total) so the show has a recognizable shape and editing stays fast. Two recurring hosts throughout: the Generalist (asks the questions a director/trustee would ask) and the Specialist (answers in plain language, one story per episode).

0:00–0:20
Cold open
One real-world line that states the stakes — no branding yet.
0:20–0:45
Intro
Show + episode title, one line on where it sits in the series.
0:45–7:30
The conversation
Generalist asks, Specialist answers; one concrete example or near-miss anecdote.
7:30–8:45
What ramsac sees
Recurring segment: a field observation that earns credibility without a sales pitch.
8:45–9:30
Recap
The single takeaway, said in one sentence, plus a next-episode teaser.
9:30–10:00
Soft CTA
One line, host-voiced: "if this sounds familiar, ramsac's happy to talk it through."

04 Season map & sequencing logic

The three verticals release as three back-to-back seasons rather than interleaved, so a new listener can find the show, binge one complete arc, and be handed straight into the next rather than waiting weeks between related episodes.

1st Cybersecurity Already the strongest-performing topic on the blog — proven demand, easiest episode 1 to get shares on. Risk exposure → everyday attacks → internal risk → proof it works → audit & act
2nd AI Rides the audience the cybersecurity season built, while interest in AI is still climbing rather than plateaued. Should we adopt → which tool → is it private → is our data ready → budget & plan

06 Miniseries II — AI

Five episodes, in order

BuildsSeries opener — the debate every leadership team is already having informally.

BuildsFrom "should we" to "which one" — the first practical decision.

Source"How confidential is ChatGPT" (referenced by Louise as a high performer — see note in §8) + "Microsoft Copilot green shield explained"

Source"AI is ready. But is your data clean, secure, and Copilot-ready?"
BuildsThe reality check after two episodes of "yes, and here's how" — the thing that actually delays rollouts.

08 Gaps & net-new episodes needed

The Granola action item was to identify gaps, not just repackage what exists. Two kinds of gap showed up while mapping the blog to episodes.

Unverified source: "How confidential is ChatGPT"

Louise named this in the meeting as a high performer, but it didn't appear on the current AI or Microsoft 365 category pages — it may be older, paginated, or retitled. Worth confirming the live URL before EP03 of the AI season is scripted, in case it's been merged into "Microsoft Copilot green shield explained" or archived ahead of the 13 July migration.

No foundational cybersecurity 101

Every existing cybersecurity article assumes some baseline (MFA, phishing, backups). For a podcast audience that may be less technical than blog readers, consider one net-new, non-blog-sourced trailer episode — "The Five Things We Assume You Already Have" — released before EP01 to level-set trustees and non-IT C-suite listeners.

No backup / disaster-recovery article

None of the nine cybersecurity articles cover backup or recovery directly, despite it being the single most common "why didn't this cost us the business" factor in real incidents. Recommend a net-new script for a future cybersecurity season 2, not squeezed into this first five.

No AI article aimed at charities specifically

Given charities/not-for-profit is one of the two named audience buckets, all nine AI articles read as generic-business. Consider one script (net-new, or adapted from EP01/EP04) that reframes the adoption and data-readiness questions for a trustee board rather than a commercial C-suite.

09 Cross-series retention mechanics

The brief asked specifically for miniseries that make people want to hear the whole run. Three mechanics do that work:

  • Episode-to-episode callbacks. Each episode's "Recap" beat ends with one line pointing at what the next episode answers ("...which is exactly why insider risk is worth a closer look — next time.") rather than a generic "thanks for listening."
  • Season finales tease the next season. EP05 of Cybersecurity closes with a 15-second teaser for the AI season; EP05 of AI teases Microsoft 365. This is the one place branding can be slightly less subtle, since it's a genuine content pointer rather than an ad.
  • A recurring segment, not just a recurring format. "What ramsac sees" in every episode is the through-line that makes the show feel authored by one team even though the topics rotate — it's also where credibility is built without a sales pitch, which is what makes the closing soft CTA land as helpful rather than promotional.
