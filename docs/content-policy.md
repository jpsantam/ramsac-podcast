# Content policy

No polarizing subject matter — no politics, geopolitics, or religion — even where a source `ramsac.com` blog post is framed that way.

**Why:** Cybersecurity EP01 was originally built around "Why tensions in the Middle East create cyber security risks for UK businesses." The geopolitical hook (Iran-aligned hacktivist groups, sanctions-themed phishing) was judged too polarizing for a client-facing, brand-safe show, even though the underlying cybersecurity content was sound.

**How to apply:** When an episode's planned source article has a political/geopolitical/religious hook, don't drop the episode — reframe it around the underlying, apolitical business or technical mechanism instead, and if needed swap in a different real blog article that makes the same point without the charged framing.

Precedent: EP01 was re-sourced from "Ethical Hacking: What Does It Mean?" instead, keeping the same "you're a target regardless of size" thesis via weak passwords / unpatched software / exposed data rather than current events.

Domestic business regulation/compliance content (e.g. referencing the UK Cyber Security and Resilience Bill) is fine as long as it stays procedural ("what it requires") rather than a debate. Apply this check to every future episode before scripting, not just ones that look obviously political at a glance.
